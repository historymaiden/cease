Having stage IV pancreatic cancer means the disease has spread to distant organs in your body and cannot be cured by surgery. At this point, your doctor has said you have around [six months to one year] left to live. Your main options are:

[Get chemotherapy], which uses drugs to treat your cancer. Most people with pancreatic cancer choose to receive chemotherapy. For these patients, the chemo is normally given by vein during weekly appointments. Depending on the severity of your cancer, chemo will likely extend your life by about [three to six months.]

[Enter Hospice], a government program that attempts to help you live the rest of your life as comfortably as possible. Entering Hospice care requires stopping active treatment. To receive the Hospice Medicare Benefit, a doctor must certify that you have about six months or less left to live.

By choosing not to pursue chemotherapy, you remain on a life expectancy trajectory of about six to 12 months. In your remaining time, your treatment will be focused on symptom relief and pain management. Many patients who choose this option do so because they believe it offers a higher quality of life and allows them to die in their own home. In most cases, Hospice nurses provide symptom management and emotional support to patients and caretakers.

Estimated cost: If you are currently on Medicare, you can receive Hospice care for free.
